Question 3

Contrary to the second quarter, third quarter has been surprisingly easy. Recall the topics of gravity, gravitation, and Kepler's planetary laws of motion, uniform circular motion, torque, rotational inertia, and center of mass.

Gravity is basically the acceleration you feel when you get near any object, it can be small or large.

UCM is the part of physics that refers to the centripetal acceleration, velocities, normal forces, and etc. of uniformly rotating systems.

Torque is the ability to apply more force by changing the location where the force is applied.

Rotational inertia is the property of spinning objects to resist change in acceleration.

Okay, so rotational inertia. A conceptual, easy, break question: ignoring air resistance and friction, what reaches the ground faster, a hollow cylinder or a solid cylinder?
